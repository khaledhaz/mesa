Why pay-at-table is still broken

Splitting a bill in 2026 still works the same way it did in 2010.

One person pays. Everyone else says "I'll send you" — and sometimes they do. Your server spends ten minutes at a table that should have turned. Your tips are lower because the process feels pressured. And if a group of eight walks in? Your staff quietly dreads it.

The QR payment platforms that were supposed to fix this mostly fixed the "tap to pay" part. The splitting problem — where everyone at the table just pays their own share, on their own phone — is still unsolved.

"Every diner at the table claims their items. Every diner pays from their own phone. Nobody has to Venmo anyone."
01

Everyone pays their own share. From their own phone.

When a table scans the Mesa QR, every diner joins the same live bill. Each person taps to claim their items — the risotto, the two glasses of wine, the dessert they're not sharing. Then they pay. Independently. No one person carries the table. No transfers after. No argument about the service charge.

This is the feature every restaurant operator has asked for. It's the one no one built — until Mesa.

Your loyalty program. At checkout. No app required.

When a guest pays, Mesa asks for their number. That's it — that's your loyalty program. Next time they sit at any of your tables, Mesa recognises them, applies their reward, and shows them how many visits they have. You get the repeat-diner data. They get recognised. Nobody downloads anything.

When your guest scans the QR, they see your logo, your colors, your dish photos. Not a payment processor's template. Not a generic white screen. The checkout feels like part of your restaurant — because it is. Full white-label, configured in your dashboard during setup.

Transparent tipping earns more trust — and more tips.

Before the tip prompt, your guest sees the full total: food, drink, service charge, everything. No surprises after they've already decided what to tip. The server's name is on screen. "No tip" is a single tap, clearly labeled, not buried. Guests tip more when they trust the process.

Mesa has an offline fallback mode. If the POS connection drops, diners can still pay by entering their table number and paying a manually-entered amount. It is not the full itemized experience, but your guests never hit a dead end.
